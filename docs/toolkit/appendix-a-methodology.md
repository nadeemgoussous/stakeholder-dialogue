# Appendix A: Methodology and Data Collection

This appendix describes the methodology used to develop the IRENA Participatory Processes for Strategic Energy Planning Toolkit.

---

## 1. Engagement Through Workshops and Events

A blend of thematic workshops, webinars, bilateral calls and participation in relevant activities provided platforms for diverse stakeholder participation.

These formats facilitated the exchange of ideas and experiences, crucial for understanding the dynamics of participatory processes in LTES.

### Engagement Statistics
- **Dozens of speakers** participated
- **Hundreds of participants** were involved in discussions

## 3. Data Collection

Information was gathered through a combination of:

### Methods Used

| Method | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| **Interactive sessions** | Engaging stakeholders in real-time discussions |
| **Literature review** | Understanding existing research and best practices |
| **Expert interviews** | Gathering insights from government officials, scientists and civil society experts |

### Expert Categories Interviewed

- Government officials responsible for energy planning and national consultation processes
- Scientists and experts from civil society

### Approach

This approach ensured a comprehensive understanding of good practices in participatory processes within the development of LTES in both government and non-governmental settings.

---

## Key Methodological Principles

1. **Collaborative Process**: The toolkit was developed through collective experiences shared within the LTES Network since 2018

2. **Emphasis on Engagement**: Activities emphasised engagement and collaboration as key to understanding participatory processes

3. **Expert Guidance**: Scientific advisory committee influenced structure and content

4. **Diverse Sources**: Combination of workshops, literature review and expert interviews ensured comprehensive coverage

5. **Practitioner Focus**: Focus on practical application for government energy planners
